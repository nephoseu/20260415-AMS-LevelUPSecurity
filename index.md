# Fabrikam – Pitch Perfect Group Activities (Extended Brief) - by Fast Lane and Nephos

This document supports group-based **Pitch Perfect** activities using the fictitious company **Fabrikam**.  
Participants work in groups (up to 5 groups, ~4 people per group) and deliver a **5-minute presentation per group**.

Each stage represents a realistic point in a **Microsoft Security sales cycle** and builds on the previous one.

---

## Stage 1: Executive Security Proposal – Pitch Perfect

- **Company Background:**  
  Fabrikam is a mid-sized US-based manufacturing company with approximately 540 employees. The business focuses on modular housing systems and prides itself on innovation and fast time-to-market. IT is run by a very lean 3‑person team supporting both office staff and the manufacturing floor.

- **Current Environment:**  
  Fabrikam uses Microsoft 365 E3 for knowledge workers and Microsoft 365 F3 for frontline staff. Security has evolved through multiple third‑party point solutions (email security, EDR, CASB), resulting in a fragmented and reactive posture.

- **What Happened:**  
  Fabrikam experienced two major security incidents in quick succession. A targeted ransomware attack nearly succeeded after phishing and lateral movement. Shortly after, a trusted senior employee exfiltrated sensitive product designs, later used by a competitor.

- **Business Impact:**  
  Although disaster was narrowly avoided, Fabrikam suffered reputational damage, lost first‑mover advantage on a key product, and fully exhausted the IT team. The CEO now sees security as existential to the business.

- **Executive Ask:**  
  The CEO needs a clear and confident proposal *today* explaining how Fabrikam can prevent ransomware, reduce insider risk, simplify security operations, and protect the company’s future.

- **Group Task:**  
  Prepare a **5‑minute executive‑level pitch** proposing a Microsoft Security approach (Microsoft Defender + Microsoft Purview). Focus on outcomes, risk reduction, and business value rather than technical depth.

- **Required Deliverables:**  
  Executive summary, high‑level solution overview, justification for Microsoft Security, and the business value Fabrikam will gain.

---

## Stage 2: Pitch‑to‑Proof – Show Me It Works

- **Situation Update:**  
  The CEO accepted your proposal conceptually but now wants proof. Leadership wants to see how Microsoft Security works in practice.

- **New Stakeholder:**  
  The CFO has joined the discussion. They are cost‑conscious and concerned about operational complexity, licensing sprawl, and time‑to‑value.

- **Customer Expectations:**  
  The demo must show how Microsoft Defender and Microsoft Purview work together to protect identities, endpoints, email, and sensitive data — including modern risks such as AI usage and data leakage.

- **Key Business Questions:**  
  How quickly can this be deployed? Will this simplify or add complexity? How does this reduce cost versus existing tools?

- **Group Task:**  
  Design a **5‑minute demo storyline** (not a click‑by‑click product demo). Describe *what* you would show and *why*, tying each element back to Fabrikam’s risks and incidents.

- **Success Criteria:**  
  The CEO leaves confident in the security transformation.  
  The CFO leaves confident in value, simplicity, and ROI.

- **Required Deliverables:**  
  Clear demo narrative, key talking points, and messaging aligned to both CEO and CFO concerns.

---

## Stage 3: Objection Handling – Compete & Defend

- **Unexpected Turn:**  
  After the demo, the CFO shares the proposal with another security vendor with whom they have a longstanding relationship.

- **Competitive Pressure:**  
  The competitor launches a FUD campaign questioning Microsoft’s security credibility, architecture complexity, and integration.

- **Customer Risk:**  
  Deal momentum slows. The CEO becomes uncertain after hearing conflicting perspectives.

- **Typical Objections:**  
  “Microsoft has security issues themselves”,  
  “This looks too complex to operate”,  
  “Point solutions are best‑of‑breed”.

- **Group Task:**  
  Prepare a **5‑minute objection‑handling pitch** that professionally counters these concerns using evidence, platform value, and business outcomes.

- **Recommended Framework:**  
  Acknowledge concern → Present evidence → Reframe as Microsoft advantage → Advance next step.

- **Required Deliverables:**  
  Clear, confident objection responses mapped to CEO trust and CFO risk concerns, demonstrating advisor‑level credibility.
